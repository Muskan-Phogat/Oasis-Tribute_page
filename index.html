Nikola Tesla

1856 – 1943
"The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine."

Biography

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian‑American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating‑current (AC) electricity supply system. His pioneering work laid the foundation for wireless communication, X‑ray imaging, and radio‑frequency control.

Throughout his life Tesla filed more than 300 patents and envisioned technologies—from wireless power transmission to autonomous drones—that were decades ahead of their time. Though often overshadowed in his era, he remains a symbol of visionary genius and relentless curiosity.

Key Milestones

1884Arrives in New York and begins working with Thomas Edison.
1888Introduces the AC induction motor and transformer at AIEE.
1891Patents the Tesla Coil and becomes a U.S. citizen.
1893Showcases wireless lighting at the Chicago World’s Fair.
1898Demonstrates radio‑controlled boat—an early unmanned vehicle.
1917Receives the Edison Medal for contributions to electrical engineering.
